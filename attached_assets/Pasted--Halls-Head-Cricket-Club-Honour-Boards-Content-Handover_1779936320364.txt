# Halls Head Cricket Club — Honour Boards Content

_Handover document generated 28 May 2026_

This file contains the finalised content for two new pages on the Halls Head Cricket Club honour boards site:

1. **A Grade Cap Register** — chronological debut order of every A Grade player
2. **Life Members** — the 14 inductees with their biographical blurbs and induction years

Both pages are intended to be added as additional tabs in the existing honour boards interface, alongside Games / Runs / Wickets / Dismissals / High Scores / Best Bowling / Centurions / 5-Wicket Hauls / Search.

---

## Implementation Context

### Existing data files in `replit_app/data/`

- `cap_register.json` — 247 cap entries, already cross-referenced against player stats
- `life_members.json` — 14 life member entries with blurbs and stats hooks
- `players.json` — all 688 club players (cleaned, Elliot Ireland removed as a data error)
- `per_grade.json` — per-grade career stats for every player

### Cap Register entry shape

```json
{
  "cap_no": 1,
  "name": "Mark Rowe",
  "deceased": false,
  "in_stats": true,
  "player_id": "rowe__mark",   // null if no stats
  "games_a_grade": 140         // 0 if no stats
}
```

### Life Member entry shape

```json
{
  "name": "Paul Gillam",
  "year": 2000,
  "player_id": "gillam__paul",  // null for non-playing members
  "is_playing_member": true,
  "blurb": "Co-founder of...",  // markdown-like paragraphs separated by \n\n
  "role": "Scorer"              // only present for non-playing members
}
```

Playing members should also have their career stats rendered alongside the blurb. Stats can be looked up via `player_id` against `players.json` and `per_grade.json`.

## A Grade Cap Register Page

### Page header

**Title:** A Grade Cap Register

**Subtitle:** Every player to wear the cap for Halls Head Cricket Club in A Grade competition, in chronological order of debut. 247 caps issued — 156 with stats on record, 4 since deceased.

### Disclaimer card (display prominently at top)

**Heading:** About this register

**Body:**

> Cap numbers are assigned in chronological order of A Grade debut for Halls Head Cricket Club. Prior to the adoption of the digital scoring platforms **MyCricket** and **PlayHQ**, the club only retained statistics for players who had played 10 or more games. As a result, many of the players in this register — particularly those from the foundational era — have no statistical record despite having earned their A Grade cap. Their place in club history is honoured here regardless.

**Legend (display below the disclaimer):**

- ✓ stats available — green tag
- ○ no stats (pre-digital era) — grey/dimmed tag
- ✝ deceased — gold marker beside the player's name

### Data notes

- Cap numbers are sequential 1–247 (no gaps). One historical typo was corrected: Freddie Higton was originally listed as cap #235 (duplicate), now correctly cap #238.
- Cap #221 was originally listed as Aaron Burrage. Aaron Burrage has never played A Grade for Halls Head — this entry has been corrected to **Andrew Pasco** who has 5 A Grade games on record.
- Cap #214 (Elliot Ireland) had a single A Grade game in the MyCricket records, but this was confirmed as a data error by the club. The stat has been removed from the player database. The cap entry is retained in the register but marked as having no stats.
- Players whose names appeared in slightly different forms across systems have been resolved (e.g. Matt vs Matthew, Mick vs Michael, spelling variants like Stanley vs Staney). The full mapping is preserved in `cap_register.json` via the `stats_name_variant` field where applicable.
- **Outstanding**: Trevor Allen appears in A Grade stats (23 games, 706 runs, 25 wickets) but is not in the cap register. This is being investigated — he may be a preferred-name variant for another listed player (potentially Wayne Allen, cap #36), or a separate person who needs their own cap number. Leave him out of the register for now pending club confirmation.

## Life Members Page

### Page header

**Title:** Life Members

**Subtitle:** Halls Head Cricket Club's highest individual honour — recognising members whose contribution to the club has been exceptional and enduring. 14 Life Members inducted since the award was first given.

### Layout per member

Each Life Member should have their own card containing:

1. **Name** (large, gold, Roboto Slab Bold) — clickable link to player profile if `player_id` is set
2. **Role label** (italic, smaller, below name) — only displayed for non-playing members (Kevin Burns shows 'Scorer')
3. **Induction year** (top right of card, prominent)
4. **Career stats grid** — for playing members only, 6 stat tiles:
   - Games | Runs (with avg + HS) | 50s/100s | Wickets (with avg + BB) | 5-Wicket Hauls | Dismissals (with c/s/ro breakdown)
5. **Grades played** — line listing all grades the player has played in
6. **Blurb** — italic block with gold left border, displaying paragraphs separated by `\n\n` in the source

### Important data notes

- The blurb field uses `\n\n` to separate paragraphs. Each paragraph should render as its own `<p>` element with appropriate vertical spacing. Avoid rendering as one solid block.
- Kevin Burns has `is_playing_member: false` and no `player_id` or stats — render only the blurb (no stats grid), with the 'Scorer' role label under his name.
- Members should be sorted by `year` (ascending), then by `name` alphabetically as a tiebreaker.

### Member entries

#### Mark Rowe (inducted 2000)

**Type:** Playing member
**Player ID:** `rowe__mark`

**Blurb:**

> Co-founder of Halls Head Cricket Club and the club's inaugural President for eight seasons (1991/92 to 1998/99). Mark also captained the inaugural A Grade side, leading them to premiership glory in 1991/92 after defeating Singleton in a semi-final still spoken of as one of the finest matches in the club's history, then comprehensively defeating Rockingham in the Grand Final. Wears cap #1. Seven premierships including six in A Grade.

#### Paul Gillam (inducted 2000)

**Type:** Playing member
**Player ID:** `gillam__paul`

**Blurb:**

> Co-founder of Halls Head Cricket Club. In the winter of 1991, Paul and fellow founding member Mark Rowe — both then playing at South Coastals Cricket Club at Falcon Oval — set about forming a new club closer to home in the rapidly growing Halls Head area. Paul served as the club's inaugural Treasurer for eight consecutive seasons (1991/92 to 1998/99) and was later Club President. On the field he won eight A Grade premierships and was named Man of the Match in both the 1994/95 and 1996/97 A Grade Grand Finals.

#### Peter Wyllie (inducted 2003)

**Type:** Playing member
**Player ID:** `wyllie__peter`

**Blurb:**

> Halls Head's most decorated cricketer and a true legend of Peel cricket. Peter has won 15 premierships for the club — twelve in A Grade and three in C Grade — captaining three consecutive A Grade premierships from 1994/95 to 1996/97. He has been Man of the Match in four A Grade Grand Finals (1991/92, 1997/98, 2001/02, 2006/07), more than any other player in club history.
>
> Peter represented the Western Australian Country XI and is a Life Member of the Peel Cricket Association — the PCA A Grade competition, the Wyllie Cup, is named in honour of Peter and his brother John Wyllie of Mandurah Cricket Club. The brothers faced each other in 13 grand finals, with Peter on the winning side in twelve. Off the field, Peter served as the club's Director of Cricket for five years and was the club's Cricketer of the Year three times.
>
> The club's player-of-the-match award is also named in his honour — the Wyllie Medal is decided by a club-wide 3-2-1 vote after every game across all grades.
