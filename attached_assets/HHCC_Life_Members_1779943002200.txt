======================================================================
HALLS HEAD CRICKET CLUB - LIFE MEMBERS
======================================================================

Generated 28 May 2026

Halls Head Cricket Club's highest individual honour - recognising
members whose contribution to the club has been exceptional and
enduring. 14 Life Members inducted since the award was first given.

Listed in order of induction year.

======================================================================

1. MARK ROWE  -  INDUCTED 2000
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Co-founder of Halls Head Cricket Club and the club's inaugural
President for eight seasons (1991/92 to 1998/99). Mark also
captained the inaugural A Grade side, leading them to premiership
glory in 1991/92 after defeating Singleton in a semi-final still
spoken of as one of the finest matches in the club's history, then
comprehensively defeating Rockingham in the Grand Final. Wears cap
#1. Seven premierships including six in A Grade.


2. PAUL GILLAM  -  INDUCTED 2000
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Co-founder of Halls Head Cricket Club. In the winter of 1991, Paul
and fellow founding member Mark Rowe — both then playing at South
Coastals Cricket Club at Falcon Oval — set about forming a new club
closer to home in the rapidly growing Halls Head area. Paul served
as the club's inaugural Treasurer for eight consecutive seasons
(1991/92 to 1998/99) and was later Club President. On the field he
won eight A Grade premierships and was named Man of the Match in
both the 1994/95 and 1996/97 A Grade Grand Finals.


3. PETER WYLLIE  -  INDUCTED 2003
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Halls Head's most decorated cricketer and a true legend of Peel
cricket. Peter has won 15 premierships for the club — twelve in A
Grade and three in C Grade — captaining three consecutive A Grade
premierships from 1994/95 to 1996/97. He has been Man of the Match
in four A Grade Grand Finals (1991/92, 1997/98, 2001/02, 2006/07),
more than any other player in club history.

Peter represented the Western Australian Country XI and is a Life
Member of the Peel Cricket Association — the PCA A Grade
competition, the Wyllie Cup, is named in honour of Peter and his
brother John Wyllie of Mandurah Cricket Club. The brothers faced
each other in 13 grand finals, with Peter on the winning side in
twelve. Off the field, Peter served as the club's Director of
Cricket for five years and was the club's Cricketer of the Year
three times.

The club's player-of-the-match award is also named in his honour —
the Wyllie Medal is decided by a club-wide 3-2-1 vote after every
game across all grades.


4. ROGER WREN  -  INDUCTED 2004
----------------------------------------------------------------------
A foundation A Grader (cap #9) who has played 291 games for Halls
Head, taking 399 wickets across A, B and C Grades with five 5-wicket
hauls (BB 7/54). Ten A Grade premierships. Off the field, Roger
served as Club President for nine consecutive seasons — a record
that stood for many years until recently equalled by Ashley Wyborn.


5. DALE BURNS  -  INDUCTED 2006
----------------------------------------------------------------------
One of the most prolific cricketers in Halls Head history. Dale has
played across all six men's grades — A through F — and is the club's
all-time leading run-scorer. Thirteen premierships including ten in
A Grade. Captained A Grade in 1997/98 and C Grade in 2016/17, and
represented both the Western Australian Country XI and the
Australian Country XI — the only player in club history to have worn
the Australian Country jersey.


6. JUSTIN LOCKYER  -  INDUCTED 2008
----------------------------------------------------------------------
A B Grade specialist who has played 204 games across B, C and E
Grades, taking 275 wickets with three 5-wicket hauls. Captained
back-to-back B Grade premierships in 2000/01 and 2001/02, winning
four B Grade flags in total. More recently he has served on the club
committee as Junior Coordinator and has coached both of his sons'
junior teams.


7. CHRIS BAKER  -  INDUCTED 2013
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The club's premier wicketkeeper, Chris has taken 273 dismissals —
202 catches, 67 stumpings, 4 run outs — across his 260-game career
spanning A through E Grades. Eleven premierships across four grades,
including four in A Grade. Chris served as the club's Treasurer in
the mid-2000s and as Junior Club President for many years.


8. DARREN JEFFREY  -  INDUCTED 2013
----------------------------------------------------------------------
A C Grade master and one of the club's all-time greats. Darren has
won five C Grade premierships, captaining two of them (2007/08,
2008/09), and has taken 404 wickets for the club — the third-most in
club history — with twelve 5-wicket hauls (BB 6/8). Named Man of the
Match in the 2008/09 C Grade Grand Final.

Off the field, Darren served as the club's Treasurer for many years
and is a Life Member of the Peel Cricket Association — the PCA C
Grade competition, the Jeffrey Cup, is named in his honour. He has
also represented Western Australia in Over-60s Veterans cricket.


9. CHRIS PHELPS  -  INDUCTED 2014
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The club's most successful cricketer in raw numbers. Chris has
played 402 games — more games than any other player in club history
— and is the club's all-time leading wicket-taker with 608 wickets,
including eleven 5-wicket hauls and club-record figures of 8/12.
Sixteen premierships — fifteen in A Grade, more than any other
player — including back-to-back A Grade captaincies in 2006/07 and
2008/09. Man of the Match in the 2008/09 A Grade Grand Final.

Chris has represented both Western Australia and Australia in
Over-40s Veterans cricket. Off the field, he coached the club's
first girls' cricket team for three seasons.


10. KEVIN BURNS  -  INDUCTED 2016
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Role: Scorer (non-playing member)

Halls Head Cricket Club's only non-playing Life Member. Kevin served
as the club's A Grade scorer for many years and went on to be the
Western Australian Country XI scorer — recognised across the state
for his meticulous record-keeping and unwavering dedication to the
game. His contribution to the club and to country cricket more
broadly has been immeasurable.


11. JAMES WOODS  -  INDUCTED 2021
----------------------------------------------------------------------
James has played 206 games across five grades, scoring 2,336 runs
(HS 94*) and taking 61 wickets with one 5-wicket haul (BB 5/31).
Captained the D Grade premiership in 2012/13 and won a second D
Grade flag in 2013/14. Off the field, James has served the club as
President, as Treasurer, and as a long-standing committee member —
three of the club's most demanding administrative roles.


12. MATTHEW GUYTON  -  INDUCTED 2021
----------------------------------------------------------------------
An all-grades servant of the club. Matt has played 356 games across
all six men's grades, taking 349 wickets (BB 6/12) and scoring 3,134
runs with one century (HS 107*). Ten premierships across A, B, C, D
and E Grades — captaining the 2013/14 C Grade and the 2025/26 E
Grade premiership sides. He has served on the club committee for
over ten years, including two years as Vice President.


13. GRANT MATTHEWS  -  INDUCTED 2026
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Grant has played 286 games for the club across all six men's grades
plus PPL, scoring 5,127 runs with one century (HS 103) and taking
227 wickets (BB 6/55). Captained back-to-back B Grade premierships
in 2015/16 and 2016/17, with six premierships in total spanning A,
B, D and E Grades. Off the field he has been a long-serving
committee member, club coach, net captain, and club sponsor — a
contributor on every front.


14. JASON YOUNG  -  INDUCTED 2026
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Jason has played 209 games for the club across five grades, scoring
4,259 runs at 30.9 with two centuries (HS 148) and taking 156
wickets at 15.5 with one 5-wicket haul. Five premierships including
the 2016/17 C Grade. Off the field, Jason has served as Junior Club
President, Senior Club Vice President, Secretary, and as coach of
both his sons' teams from Under 10 right through to Under 17 —
sometimes coaching both teams in the same year.
